A ==**morphism of Lie Algebras**== is a linear map $T:\fg\to \mathfrak{h}$ preserving the Lie bracket.

The assignment $\fg = T_1G$ is called $Lie(G)$, the ==**Lie algebra of $G$**==. This is a functor from the category of Lie groups to the category of Lie algebras.

# Abelian things
- We say $x$ and $y$ ==**commute**== if $[x,y] = 0$.
- A Lie algebra is ==**abelian**== if $[\fg, \fg] = 0$.
- We call a vector subspace $\hh\subset \fg$ an ==**Lie subalgebra**== if $[\hh, \hh]\subset \hh$.
- We call $\hh$ a ==**Lie ideal**== if $[\fg, \hh]\subset \hh$.
